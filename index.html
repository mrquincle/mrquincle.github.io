Welcome to my webpage!

This is an overview of some of my code created over the years. I like research most, especially with respect to using or creating exciting new artificial intelligence methods. However, programming is just as much fun. I started like a nerd is supposed to, when I was 10 or 11, breaking the computer of my father because I was playing too much with DOS extended memory settings and solved puzzles in children magazins using QBasic.

Algorithms

You can see an overview on the algorithms we have been working on at http://almende.github.com/nets/. This ranges from signal processing, classification, to planning and reasoning. One example is the well-known particle filter, another belief propagation.

Research in several European and Dutch projects, especially with the Replicator/Symbrion robots, led to a spinoff in robotics and smart systems in general, called DoBots. The blog on DoBots is regularly updated with information on algorithms and more.

In robotics SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) is of course a very important algorithm. Currently (2014) we are working on 3D SLAM mainly. Personally I am working on the application of nonparametric Bayesian methods to come up with CAD-like representations of man-made environments (without a lot of the clutter and noise). Besides that I am working on indoor localization with a BLE (Bluetooth Low-Energy) network.
